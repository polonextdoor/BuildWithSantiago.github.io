Case Study: WCSU Career Success Center Website

Project Overview

The Career Success Center at Western Connecticut State University (WCSU) needed a modern, user-friendly website to help students access resources on career readiness, job searching, and internships. I was responsible for building a site that is visually engaging, functional, and easy for staff to maintain.

Website: wcsu.edu/careersuccess

Goals

  • Improve accessibility and align with WCAG standards.
  • Streamline navigation and information hierarchy.
  • Highlight tools like PathwayU, Candid Career, The Forage, Parker Dewey, and Big Interview.
  • Maintain consistency with WCSU branding.

My Role

  • Designed the site structure and navigation flow.
  • Developed custom page layouts using HTML, CSS, and WordPress templates.
  • Collaborated with staff to gather and prioritize content.
  • Optimized mobile responsiveness and performance.
  • Ensured search engine optimization (SEO).

Process

  1. Discovery & Planning: Identified student needs and built wireframes.
  2. Design & Development: Created accessible layouts with consistent grids, fonts, and call-to-action cards.
  3. Testing & Launch: Performed mobile/cross-browser testing and iterated on feedback.

Outcome

  • Students can access career resources in fewer clicks.
  • Staff reported fewer email inquiries due to clearer navigation.
  • The site supports over 5,000 students annually in their career journeys.

Key Takeaways

  • Accessibility and usability are as important as design aesthetics.
  • Collaboration with non-technical stakeholders builds stronger outcomes.
  • Designing for scalability ensures future growth and updates.
