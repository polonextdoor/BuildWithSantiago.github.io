Case Study: WELL Initiative at WCSU

Project Overview

The Workforce Education & Lifelong Learning (WELL) Initiative is part of Western Connecticut State University’s School of Graduate, International, and Career Studies. It offers non-credit certificates, micro-credentials, and professional development programs designed to meet workforce needs and support lifelong learners.

Website: wcsu.edu/sgics/workforce-education-and-lifelong-learning

Goals

  • Clearly communicate WELL’s offerings: non-credit certificates, micro-credentials, and pathways.
  • Align the site with WCSU’s branding and the SGICS identity.
  • Provide an intuitive user experience for professionals, adult learners, and community members.
  • Ensure mobile responsiveness and accessibility for a broad audience.
  • Make content easy to update as new programs launch.

My Role

  • Structured the site’s information architecture for clarity and flow.
  • Designed modular layouts for programs, objectives, and offerings.
  • Developed the site using HTML, CSS, and WCSU’s CMS templates.
  • Optimized typography, color contrast, and spacing for accessibility.
  • Collaborated with stakeholders to refine messaging and presentation.

Process

  1. Discovery & Planning: Collected requirements, audited existing content, and wireframed key pages.
  2. Design & Development: Built layouts with modular cards for certificates and clear “Read More” calls-to-action.
  3. Testing & Launch: Performed cross-browser and mobile testing, integrated stakeholder feedback, and deployed on WCSU’s infrastructure.

Outcome

  • The WELL site makes objectives, offerings, and enrollment options immediately clear.
  • Users can easily navigate certificates, micro-credentials, and learning pathways.
  • Staff can add new programs without redesigning entire pages.
  • The initiative strengthens WCSU’s role in supporting regional workforce development.

Key Takeaways

  • Accessibility and clarity are essential when building for diverse learners.
  • Modular structures make the site scalable and easy to update.
  • Good design balances visual appeal with functional content organization.
  • Stakeholder collaboration was crucial in aligning academic goals with web usability.
